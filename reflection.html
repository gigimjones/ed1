Matchbox Replica Reflection

This reflection is all about my first 3D printed project where I had to replicate a matchbox car.

Guidelines:

The guidelines for this project were: Design a car as an intersection of three forms, design the car in a 1:1 ratio, have a size accuracy of Â±1%, export the design as an stl file, orient a model in Cura for 3D printing to minimize support material, export the file as a code, and share the final file out as an embedded STL file on a HTML webpage.

Process:

I first started by choosing a car to replicate. Once I picked my car, I then began to take measurements using calipers. I measured the length, width, and height of my car. Next, I began to draw. I used graphing paper to make the drawing as realistic as possible. I drew views of the front, side, and top as well as a three demential view so that I could see my measurements more easily. Next, I began the process of putting my design into OnShape following along with a series of tutorial videos. I started with the side view and then continued onto the front and top views so that all of the drawings were in 3D form in OnShape. Next, I used the draw feature to draw the outline of the car. I then deleted my drawing so that the outline was all that was left and my drawing was transformed into a 3 dimensional car. I then imported this design into Ultimaker and began printing. The printing took about two hours. Once my car was printed, I used tweezers and a file to get rid of the supports and make my car's shape more clear. Finally, I photographed my final design.

Setbacks and Solutions:

I struggled with my final design file in Ultimaker. I didn't change my measurements in OnShape so the file did not convert. I fixed this by going back and importing my file again. I also had help from my classmates and teachers.
